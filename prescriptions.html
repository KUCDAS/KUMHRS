Here’s what you can generally expect to find on a prescription: Patient Information: This includes the patient's full name, and sometimes their address, age, or date of birth. Date of Issue: The date on which the prescription was written. Medication Name and Strength: The specific name of the drug prescribed and the concentration or strength of the medication. Dosage Instructions: Directions on how to take the medication, including the amount per dose, the frequency of doses per day, and the duration for which the medication should be taken. Quantity: The amount of medication to be dispensed, which could be specified in number of pills, volume, or other measures. Refills: Information on whether the prescription can be refilled and, if so, how many times. Prescriber Information: The name, address, and contact details of the doctor or healthcare provider who wrote the prescription, along with their signature. The prescriber’s professional registration number may also be included. DEA Number: Required in the U.S. for prescriptions involving controlled substances, this is a number assigned to the prescriber by the Drug Enforcement Administration. Some prescriptions, particularly in electronic form, may also include barcodes or other machine-readable codes to facilitate processing and dispensing.
